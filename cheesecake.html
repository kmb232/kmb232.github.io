LIMONCELLO CHEESECAKE

This is a version of the German Quark Cheesecake recipe on thespruceeats.com. I'd had plenty of quark cheesecakes while living in Germany and thought the light texture of one would be the perfect match for the taste of limoncello.

You might have to go to your local organic market, Whole Foods, or German/Central European deli to find quark. As for the cookies for the crust, any European-style cookie with a light flavor should work. The last time I made this I used French butter cookies and it was excellent, but I've also had luck with Italian amarettis.

    Ingredients
  • For the Crust -
    • heaping cup cookie crumbs (see note above)
    • 1 cup slivered almonds
    • 3 tablespoons sugar
    • 6 tablespoons melted butter
  • For the Cheesecake -
    • 27 ounces quark
    • 8 oz. cream cheese
    • 1 cup sugar
    • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
    • 3 large eggs, separated
    • zest of 2 lemons
    • 1/2 cup limoncello
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1/2 cup flour

Preheat your oven to 350. For the crust, put your cookie crumbs and slivered almonds in a food processor and blitz until fine. Add the sugar and melted butter and continue in the food processor for a few more seconds. Spread your crust mix out in a 10 in springform pan with foil wrapped around the outside. Use a glass or other flat, circular surface to pack it in and get it to stick about 1 inch up the sides of your pan. Bake for about 10-12 minutes until the color has started to darken slightly. Note: Don't get discouraged! For something that sounds so simple, I've had incredible difficulty getting crusts like this just right. It takes practice to get a feel for that sweet spot ratio of butter to stuff.

In a large bowl, mix together the quark, cream cheese, and sugar until smooth. While that's mixing, separate the eggs and whip the egg whites in a small bowl until stiff.

Once the quark mix is nice and smooth, add the egg yoks and mix until blended. Mix in the zest, limoncello, and vanilla extract. The last thing you want to mix in is the flour. The final step is to gently fold in your egg whites.

Place in the lower third of your oven and bake for 50-60 minutes. Once it's done (the edges should be started to get a little golden and there might be a few cracks forming) turn the oven off, and leave it to cool with the oven door cracked. Once it's cool, keep in the refrigerator until ready to serve.

If desired, you can make some limoncello syrup (cook down some limoncello with a tablespoon of sugar added in) or I can heartily recommend an accompanying shot of limoncello.
